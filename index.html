Not because the product is wrong. Because the opening is about the sender, not the prospect.

Rule #1
The first sentence must be about the prospect's world — not yours.
Their company, their role, their situation, their pain. The moment an email opens with "My name is X" or "I hope this finds you well," it's already lost. 8 email frameworks

Pick the framework.Get the email.

Eight proven cold email frameworks — each generates a structurally different email. Select the one that fits the prospect and the situation.

PAS
Problem-Agitate-Solve. Names the pain, makes it feel real, positions your solution.
AIDA
Attention-Interest-Desire-Action. Classic copywriting framework adapted for cold email.
Pattern Interrupt
An unexpected opening that breaks the template the prospect expects to see.
Social Proof Lead
Opens with a relevant customer result. Let someone else make the case first.
Question Hook
A specific question they can't not answer. Creates a loop that needs closing.
Insight Lead
Opens with a counterintuitive insight about their industry. Establishes credibility immediately.
Brevity
3–4 lines total. Sometimes the shortest email gets the highest reply rate.
Trigger Event
Built around a specific reason to reach out now — funding, hiring, post, announcement.
